Travel Info

NatureBridge, our home base for the weekend, is located at 111 Barnes Point Rd. Port Angeles, WA 98363

Flying into SeaTac

Driving from Seattle-Tacoma Airport takes approximately 2.5-3 hours.
You'll have a couple options for driving routes. You can choose to drive around the peninsula or you can take a car ferry - either from Seattle to Bainbridge Island or from Edmonds to Kingston. It takes about the same amount of time, but you'll cut down on physical driving time and will be on a boat for a bit (you can pretend you're on Grey's Anatomy and stare broodily at the city skyline if you like), so that's pretty fun.

Flying into Paine Field

If you're extra adventurous and live in a city that offers flights to the newly-converted-to-commercial-airport Paine Field, you can skip out on having to drive through Downtown Seattle. According to Google, drive time is still about 3 hours, so choose whichever flight is cheapest I guess. Or, if you like new things, do this one. Commercial flights to Paine Field start in Feb 2019 so they should have all the kinks worked out by June.

Carpooling

Either way, we recommend coordinating flights and carpools with friends. There's plenty of parking at NatureBridge but road trips are best with friends to share snacks and podcasts with and also to make sure you don't fall asleep at the wheel.
